Review new documents in Raw Documents/ and classify each into the appropriate subfolder.

For each new file, decide the best subfolder based on what the document IS:
- Pleadings: filings WITH the court (motions, briefs, complaints, answers, oppositions, replies)
- Court Documents: filings BY the court (orders, scheduling orders, hearing notices, clerk notices)
- Correspondence: letters/emails between counsel, parties, clients, agencies
- Client Documents: leases, ledgers, contracts, photos, records provided by the client
- Drafts: works-in-progress not yet filed or sent
- Research: case law, statutes, articles, memos
- Miscellaneous: anything that doesn't fit cleanly

Do NOT move files into Email Correspondence/ — that folder is managed automatically by Rocky for email body PDFs. Only classify attachments and other documents from Raw Documents/.

For court e-filing attachments (from Tyler Technologies / CaseFileXpress):
- Documents filed BY a party (motions, complaints, answers) → Pleadings/
- Documents issued BY the court (orders, scheduling notices) → Court Documents/

If this case folder uses different subfolder names, map to the closest available match.

Pick a clean, descriptive suggested_name for each file (e.g., "Order Denying Motion to Dismiss" not "20260502T1714_ac67178c_order.pdf").

Flag any deadlines, hearing dates, or response-due dates you find in the documents.
